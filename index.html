About Me

Data-Driven Energy Researcher

I am Yash Pandey, a PhD researcher at the Department of Electrical & Electronic Engineering, Shizuoka University (Hamamatsu Campus), Japan, under Prof. Hironobu Matsuo. My research focuses on developing simplified yet robust capacity estimation models for PV and Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) in rural hybrid microgrids.

With 3+ years in Python, MATLAB, HOMER Pro, and large-scale time-series analysis, I bridge the gap between complex energy simulations and actionable planning tools for data-scarce regions. My ABP full-scholarship-funded work spans field visits to India, research exchanges in Taiwan, and publications in Wiley, Elsevier, De Gruyter, and IEEE.

Field Research

Talgao Village, Uttar Pradesh, India

February 2026 40kW Microgrid TPRMG Socio-Economic Study

In February 2026, I conducted an on-site socio-economic impact assessment of a 40kW standalone solar microgrid in Talgao Village, Sitapur District, Uttar Pradesh — operated by Tata Power Renewable Microgrid Limited (TPRMG).

The visit provided ground-truth validation for my theoretical sizing models, gathering real load data, tariff structures, and direct beneficiary feedback. This field engagement is central to my research philosophy: engineering must serve real communities, not just equations.

The findings directly informed my PhD chapter on adaptive hybrid renewable energy microgrids, integrating socio-economic growth modelling with techno-economic optimization and AI-driven predictive maintenance.
